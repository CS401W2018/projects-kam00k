  1. Block tags always start on a new line, while inline tags do not.
    Block tags will also go as far left and right as possible, while inline tags
    are confined to the bounds of their parent elements.
  2. Padding clears a transparent area around the content.
    Borders go around padding and content, are non-transparent, and can have different styles.
    Margin clears an transparent area around the borders, padding and content.
  3. There are many units of measurement available in CSS, split into two groups: absolute and relative
    Relative units are things like %, em, ch and rem, while absolute units are things like
    cm, mm, in, px, pt and pc.
  4. Figures are used for self-contained pieces of content that are relative to the
    main flow of content, but on which the flow of content does not depend.
