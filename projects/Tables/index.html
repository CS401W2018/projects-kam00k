What does this chart mean?

Solar panels come in all shapes and sizes (Bewilderingly, some are even triangular). Of course, it is good to be able to fit as much power in a given space as possible, and so the dimensions of different modules should be taken into account when designing an array. Sometimes you have enough space on a roof to squeeze in perhaps not another row, but the same number of panels of a slightly larger size and higher power rating. This chart is to help remember some of the ones we use, since memorizing every module's dimensions is a bit of a stretch. That said, this is a chart of information burned into my head that I use on an almost daily basis.

I imagine I'll be remembering that a SIL-370-HC is 40.8" x 69.4" for the rest of my life.

I have no idea why we use decimals when measuring in inches. It's an American thing.
