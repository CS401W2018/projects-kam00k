How do I make my own pixelart?

Unlike many other art media, the barrier to entry for making pixelart is rock-bottom. After all, you're just painting with squares! All you need to get started is a paint program and a mouse.

That said, getting good at making pixelart takes practice, just like any other skill. The sky is the limit for how skilled you want to become. Luckily, you are on the internet, which is absolutely brimming with tutorials and resources of every kind to help you make your blocky visions come to life.

Tools

You can use any paint program you want, and it doesn't have to be costly or complicated. Since pixelart is the very essence of "less is more", a program that lends itself to simplicity will be all the better. Here are a couple of suggestions.

MS Paint

The art tool of choice for every five-year-old furnished with a mouse. This is on almost every computer known to man and costs nothing. It is rather barebones and isn't designed for pixelart specifically, but if you want to draw and you want to do it now, now now, this can be an option.

Aseprite

Aseprite is a great tool, made specifically for the production of pixelart and pixelart animations. The two animations you see at the top of this page were made in Aseprite by myself. It is a paid product, but is source-available, so it can be obtained for free if you are willing to compile the source code yourself. A fully open source, albeit deprecated version can be found in Libresprite.

Reference Art!

When starting out, you shouldn't be drawing from imagination... and you don't have to! There are so many examples and styles of pixelart to choose from that you can reference for your own practice. There are even websites that contain sprites and environment assets that are CC0 or Public Domain which you can use, modify or redistribute freely.

Reject Modernity: Embrace Tradition

Remember the bit on the history page about cross-stitching? It turns out that cross-stitching is still a thing! This is a way you can very easily take a piece of pixelart you like and turn it into something real! A pixel is a square, as is a cross-stitch. As long as you're not changing the resolution of the image, the only challenge in cross-stitching is color-matching.

A great online utility to produce cross-stitch charts from images is Stitchfiddle.
